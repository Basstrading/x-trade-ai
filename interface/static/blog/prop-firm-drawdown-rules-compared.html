The Hidden Traps

Topstep: No Daily Loss Limit = No Safety Net

Since August 2024, Topstep removed the daily loss limit. Your only protection is the $2,000 trailing drawdown. One bad session and the account is dead. This is why you need your own daily limit — our risk desk imposes one automatically.

Apex 4.0: Everything Changed in March 2026

Apex did a complete overhaul. Drawdowns reduced, contracts cut, consistency changed from 30% to 50%. The $250K and $300K accounts are gone. If you're using old Apex numbers, you're trading with wrong limits.

Bulenox Option 1: Tick-by-Tick Trailing is Brutal

Option 1 has no daily limit and full contract access, which sounds great. But the drawdown trails every single tick, including intraday wicks. A 3-second wick can trigger the drawdown even if the trade comes back green. This is the most aggressive drawdown type of all prop firms.

TakeProfitTrader: EOD in Eval, Intraday in PRO

This is the biggest trap. During evaluation, the drawdown is EOD — forgiving. Once you pass and get a PRO account, it switches to intraday trailing — much stricter. Many traders pass the eval easily then blow the PRO because they don't adjust.

Tradeify: Scaling Starts You Small

Tradeify gives you full contracts during evaluation but starts you at 2 contracts when funded. You unlock more as your equity grows. Many traders don't realize this and plan their funded strategy around full size.

Every prop firm rule is designed to make it statistically harder for you to reach payout. Understanding these rules — and having a system that enforces them for you — is the difference between the 94% who fail and the 6% who get paid.
